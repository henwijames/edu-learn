Integral Calculus

Explore the world of integration, from basic antiderivatives to complex applications in physics and engineering.

Definite and Indefinite Integrals

Understanding Integration

Integration is the reverse process of differentiation. It helps us find areas, volumes, and many other quantities that accumulate over time or space.

Key Concepts:

  • Antiderivatives
  • Definite integrals
  • Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  • Properties of integrals

Example:

Evaluate ∫(2x + 3)dx

Solution:

  1. Find antiderivative: x² + 3x + C
  2. For definite integral, evaluate at bounds

Integration Techniques

Advanced Integration Methods

Learn various techniques to solve complex integrals, from substitution to integration by parts.

Key Techniques:

  • U-substitution
  • Integration by parts
  • Partial fractions
  • Trigonometric substitution

Example:

Evaluate ∫x·e^x dx using integration by parts

Solution:

  1. Let u = x, dv = e^x dx
  2. Find du = dx, v = e^x
  3. Apply formula: uv - ∫v du
  4. Result: x·e^x - e^x + C

Applications of Integration

Real-World Applications

Discover how integration is used to solve practical problems in various fields.

Applications:

  • Area between curves
  • Volume of revolution
  • Work and energy
  • Center of mass

Example:

Find the area between y = x² and y = 2x from x = 0 to x = 2

Solution:

  1. Set up integral: ∫(2x - x²)dx from 0 to 2
  2. Evaluate: [x² - x³/3] from 0 to 2
  3. Calculate: 4 - 8/3 = 4/3
