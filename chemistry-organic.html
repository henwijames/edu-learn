Organic Chemistry

Explore the fascinating world of carbon-based compounds, their structures, reactions, and applications in life and industry.

Structure and Bonding

Carbon Compounds

Understand the unique properties of carbon and how it forms the basis of organic molecules.

Key Concepts:

  • Hybridization (sp³, sp², sp)
  • Sigma and pi bonds
  • Molecular geometry
  • Resonance structures

Example:

Draw the Lewis structure and determine the hybridization of carbon in CH₄, C₂H₄, and C₂H₂.

Solution:

  1. CH₄: sp³ hybridization, tetrahedral geometry
  2. C₂H₄: sp² hybridization, trigonal planar geometry
  3. C₂H₂: sp hybridization, linear geometry

Functional Groups

Common Organic Groups

Learn about the characteristic groups that determine the properties and reactivity of organic compounds.

Key Groups:

  • Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
  • Alcohols and ethers
  • Aldehydes and ketones
  • Carboxylic acids and esters

Example:

Identify the functional groups in CH₃CH₂OH, CH₃CHO, and CH₃COOH.

Solution:

  1. CH₃CH₂OH: Alcohol (-OH)
  2. CH₃CHO: Aldehyde (-CHO)
  3. CH₃COOH: Carboxylic acid (-COOH)

Reaction Mechanisms

Organic Reactions

Understand how organic compounds react and the mechanisms behind these transformations.

Key Mechanisms:

  • Substitution reactions
  • Elimination reactions
  • Addition reactions
  • Oxidation and reduction

Example:

Predict the product of the reaction between CH₃CH=CH₂ and HBr.

Solution:

  1. Markovnikov's rule applies
  2. H adds to carbon with more H atoms
  3. Product: CH₃CHBrCH₃
