Quantum Mechanics

Wave-Particle Duality

Discover how particles can behave as waves and waves as particles, challenging our classical understanding of physics.

Key Concepts:

  • Wave-particle duality
  • Heisenberg uncertainty principle
  • Schrödinger equation
  • Quantum tunneling

Example:

Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron moving at 1×10⁶ m/s.

Solution:

  1. Use λ = h/p = h/mv
  2. λ = (6.63×10⁻³⁴)/(9.11×10⁻³¹ × 1×10⁶)
  3. λ = 7.28×10⁻¹⁰ m

Special Relativity

Space and Time

Learn how Einstein's theory of relativity revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy.

Key Concepts:

  • Time dilation
  • Length contraction
  • Mass-energy equivalence
  • Lorentz transformations

Example:

A spaceship travels at 0.8c relative to Earth. How much time passes on Earth when 1 year passes on the ship?

Solution:

  1. Use time dilation formula: t = t₀/√(1-v²/c²)
  2. t = 1/√(1-0.8²)
  3. t = 1.67 years

Nuclear Physics

Atomic Nuclei and Radioactivity

Explore the structure of atomic nuclei, radioactive decay, and nuclear reactions.

Key Concepts:

  • Nuclear binding energy
  • Radioactive decay
  • Nuclear fission and fusion
  • Half-life calculations

Example:

A radioactive isotope has a half-life of 5 days. How much of a 100g sample remains after 15 days?

Solution:

  1. Number of half-lives = 15/5 = 3
  2. Remaining amount = 100 × (1/2)³
  3. Remaining amount = 12.5g
