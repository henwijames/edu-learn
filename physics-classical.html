Classical Mechanics

Master the fundamental principles of motion, forces, and energy in classical physics.

Newton's Laws of Motion

Fundamental Principles

Understand the three laws that form the foundation of classical mechanics and govern the motion of objects.

Key Concepts:

  • First Law: Inertia
  • Second Law: Force and Acceleration
  • Third Law: Action and Reaction
  • Free-body diagrams

Example:

A 2kg block is pulled with a force of 10N on a frictionless surface. What is its acceleration?

Solution:

  1. Use F = ma
  2. 10N = 2kg × a
  3. a = 5 m/s²

Work, Energy, and Power

Energy Conservation

Learn about different forms of energy and how they transform while the total energy remains constant.

Key Concepts:

  • Kinetic energy
  • Potential energy
  • Conservation of energy
  • Power and efficiency

Example:

A 1kg ball is dropped from 10m height. What is its velocity just before hitting the ground?

Solution:

  1. Initial PE = mgh = 1 × 9.8 × 10 = 98J
  2. Final KE = ½mv² = 98J
  3. v = √(2 × 98) = 14 m/s

Momentum and Collisions

Conservation of Momentum

Explore how momentum is conserved in collisions and how it relates to forces and motion.

Key Concepts:

  • Linear momentum
  • Impulse
  • Elastic collisions
  • Inelastic collisions

Example:

Two balls of mass 2kg and 3kg collide elastically. The first ball moves at 4 m/s, the second at 2 m/s. Find their final velocities.

Solution:

  1. Conserve momentum: m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = m₁v₁' + m₂v₂'
  2. Conserve energy: ½m₁v₁² + ½m₂v₂² = ½m₁v₁'² + ½m₂v₂'²
