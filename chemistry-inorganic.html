Inorganic Chemistry

Discover the properties and behavior of inorganic compounds, from simple salts to complex coordination compounds.

Periodic Table Trends

Element Properties

Learn how the properties of elements change across the periodic table and how these trends affect chemical behavior.

Key Concepts:

  • Atomic radius
  • Ionization energy
  • Electronegativity
  • Electron affinity

Example:

Compare the atomic radii of Na, Mg, and Al, and explain the trend.

Solution:

  1. Na > Mg > Al
  2. Across a period, atomic radius decreases
  3. Due to increasing nuclear charge

Chemical Bonding

Types of Bonds

Understand the different types of chemical bonds and how they determine the properties of inorganic compounds.

Key Concepts:

  • Ionic bonding
  • Covalent bonding
  • Metallic bonding
  • Intermolecular forces

Example:

Predict the type of bond in NaCl, HCl, and Fe.

Solution:

  1. NaCl: Ionic bond
  2. HCl: Polar covalent bond
  3. Fe: Metallic bond

Coordination Compounds

Complex Ions

Explore the structure and properties of coordination compounds, including their applications in industry and biology.

Key Concepts:

  • Ligands and coordination number
  • Crystal field theory
  • Isomerism in complexes
  • Applications in catalysis

Example:

Draw the structure of [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺ and determine its coordination number.

Solution:

  1. Octahedral geometry
  2. Coordination number = 6
  3. NH₃ ligands arranged in octahedral fashion
