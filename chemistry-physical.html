Physical Chemistry

Explore the fundamental principles that govern chemical systems, from thermodynamics to quantum mechanics.

Thermodynamics

Energy and Equilibrium

Learn about the laws of thermodynamics and how they govern chemical reactions and physical processes.

Key Concepts:

  • First and Second Laws
  • Enthalpy and Entropy
  • Gibbs Free Energy
  • Chemical Equilibrium

Example:

Calculate ΔG for a reaction with ΔH = -50 kJ/mol and ΔS = 100 J/mol·K at 298K.

Solution:

  1. ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
  2. ΔG = -50 kJ/mol - (298K × 0.1 kJ/mol·K)
  3. ΔG = -50 kJ/mol - 29.8 kJ/mol
  4. ΔG = -79.8 kJ/mol

Chemical Kinetics

Reaction Rates

Understand how chemical reactions proceed and the factors that influence their rates.

Key Concepts:

  • Rate laws
  • Reaction mechanisms
  • Activation energy
  • Catalysis

Example:

For a first-order reaction with k = 0.05 min⁻¹, calculate the time required for 75% completion.

Solution:

  1. ln[A] = ln[A]₀ - kt
  2. ln(0.25) = ln(1) - (0.05)t
  3. -1.386 = 0 - 0.05t
  4. t = 27.7 minutes

Quantum Chemistry

Molecular Structure

Explore the quantum mechanical principles that determine molecular structure and properties.

Key Concepts:

  • Wave functions
  • Molecular orbitals
  • Spectroscopy
  • Electronic transitions

Example:

Calculate the energy of a photon with wavelength 500 nm.

Solution:

  1. E = hc/λ
  2. E = (6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s × 3 × 10⁸ m/s) / (500 × 10⁻⁹ m)
  3. E = 3.98 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
  4. E = 2.48 eV
