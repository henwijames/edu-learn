Electric Fields and Forces

Electric Charge and Fields

Understand the nature of electric charge and how it creates electric fields that influence other charges.

Key Concepts:

  • Coulomb's Law
  • Electric field lines
  • Electric potential
  • Gauss's Law

Example:

Calculate the electric field at a point 2m from a point charge of 5μC.

Solution:

  1. Use E = kQ/r²
  2. E = (9×10⁹ × 5×10⁻⁶)/2²
  3. E = 11,250 N/C

Magnetic Fields and Forces

Magnetism and Magnetic Fields

Learn about magnetic fields, their sources, and how they interact with moving charges and currents.

Key Concepts:

  • Magnetic field lines
  • Lorentz force
  • Biot-Savart Law
  • Ampere's Law

Example:

A proton moves at 3×10⁶ m/s perpendicular to a 0.5T magnetic field. What is the force on the proton?

Solution:

  1. Use F = qvB
  2. F = (1.6×10⁻¹⁹)(3×10⁶)(0.5)
  3. F = 2.4×10⁻¹³ N

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday's Law and Applications

Discover how changing magnetic fields create electric fields and how this principle powers our modern world.

Key Concepts:

  • Faraday's Law
  • Lenz's Law
  • AC generators
  • Transformers

Example:

A coil with 100 turns has a magnetic flux of 0.5Wb. If the flux changes to 0.2Wb in 0.1s, what is the induced EMF?

Solution:

  1. Use ε = -N(ΔΦ/Δt)
  2. ε = -100(0.2-0.5)/0.1
  3. ε = 300V
