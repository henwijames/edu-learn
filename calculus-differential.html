Limits and Continuity

Understanding Limits

A limit describes the value that a function approaches as the input approaches some value. It's the foundation of calculus and helps us understand behavior at points where direct evaluation isn't possible.

Key Concepts:

  • One-sided limits
  • Infinite limits
  • Limits at infinity
  • Squeeze theorem

Example:

Find the limit of f(x) = (x² - 1)/(x - 1) as x approaches 1

Solution:

  1. Factor the numerator: (x - 1)(x + 1)
  2. Simplify: (x - 1)(x + 1)/(x - 1) = x + 1
  3. Take the limit: lim(x→1) (x + 1) = 2

Derivatives and Differentiation

Introduction to Derivatives

The derivative represents the rate of change of a function at any point. It's a fundamental tool for understanding how quantities change with respect to each other.

Key Concepts:

  • Definition of derivative
  • Power rule
  • Product rule
  • Quotient rule

Example:

Find the derivative of f(x) = x³ + 2x² - 5x + 3

Solution:

  1. Apply power rule to each term
  2. f'(x) = 3x² + 4x - 5

Applications of Derivatives

Real-World Applications

Derivatives have numerous applications in physics, engineering, economics, and other fields. They help us understand rates of change and optimize various processes.

Applications:

  • Velocity and acceleration
  • Related rates problems
  • Optimization
  • Curve sketching

Example:

A ladder 10 feet long rests against a vertical wall. If the bottom of the ladder slides away from the wall at a rate of 1 ft/s, how fast is the top of the ladder sliding down the wall when the bottom is 6 feet from the wall?

Solution:

  1. Use the Pythagorean theorem: x² + y² = 100
  2. Differentiate with respect to time
  3. Solve for dy/dt when x = 6
